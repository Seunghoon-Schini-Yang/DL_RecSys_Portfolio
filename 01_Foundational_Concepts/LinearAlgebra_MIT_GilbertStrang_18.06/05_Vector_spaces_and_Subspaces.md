### ¶ Vector Spaces ¶

Vector spaces has to be closed under <b>multiplication and addition</b> of vectors.
<br>In other words, <b>linear combinations</b>.

- Ex_1<br>
The first quadrant ($Q1$) is not a vector space.<br>
It's not closed under multiplication by all real numbers.<br>
$(-1) * (3, 2) = (-3, -2) \not\in Q1$ 
- Ex_2<br>
$R^{2}$ Space is a vector space.
<br>

### ¶ Subspaces ¶

Every subspace has got to contain <b>zero vector.</b>
- Ex : Subspaces of $R^{2}$
    1. $R^{2}$ itself
    2. Any line through zero vector
    3. Zero vector
<br>

For subspaces $S$ and $T$, intersection $S \cap T$ is also a subspace.
```math
\begin{align*}
\text{When two vectors}\quad \vec{v}, \vec{w} \quad \text{in}\quad S \cap T \\
\text{then}\quad \begin{rcases}
a\vec{v}+b\vec{w} \in S \\
a\vec{v}+b\vec{w} \in T
\end{rcases} \quad\text{(Linear Combinations)} \\
\text{So,}\quad a\vec{v}+b\vec{w} \in S \cap T
\end{align*}
```
