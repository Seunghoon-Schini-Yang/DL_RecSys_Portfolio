**â—‹ Eigenvectors**

$Ax$ parallel to $x$

```math
A\vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x}
```

If $A$ is <b>singular</b>, then $\lambda = 0$ is <b>eigenvalue</b>.

What are the $\vec{x}$'s and $\lambda$'s for projection matrix $P$?

Any $\vec{x}$ in the plane : $P\vec{x} = \vec{x} \quad (\lambda = 1)$

Any $\vec{x} \perp$ (plane) : $P\vec{x} = \vec{0} \quad (\lambda = 0)$

Permutation matrix

```math
\begin{align}
A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \\

\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\
A\vec{x} = \vec{x} \quad (\lambda = 1) \\

\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\
A\vec{x} = -\vec{x} \quad (\lambda = -1) \\
\end{align}
```

Fact : Sum of the $\lambda$'s = $a_{11} + a_{22} + \cdots + a_{nn}$ (Sum of the diagonal entries)

How to solve $A\vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x}$


```math
\begin{align}
A\vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x} \\
(A - \lambda I)\vec{x} = \vec{0} (A - \lambda I must be singular) \\
\det (A - \lambda I) = 0

\end{align}
```


```math
\begin{align}
A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \\

\det (A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 3-\lambda & 1 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{vmatrix}  = (3-\lambda)^{2} - 1 = \lambda^{2} - 6\lambda + 8 = (\lambda-4)(\lambda-2) \\
\lambda_{1} = 4, \vec{x}_{1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\
\lambda_{2} = 2, \vec{x}_{2} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
```

If $A\vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x}$,
then $(A+3I)\vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x} + 3\vec{x} = (\lambda + 3)\vec{x}$

and $B$ has eigenvalues $\alpha$, $B\vec{x} = \alpha \vec{x}$
then $(A+B)\vec{x} = (\lambda+\alpha)\vec{x}$. : False

90 degrees Rotation matrix $Q$ (orthogonal)

```math
\begin{align}
Q = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \\
trace = 0 + 0 = \lambda_{1} + \lambda_{2} \\
det = 1 = \lambda_{1}\lambda_{2}
\end{align}
```

```math
\begin{align}
\det (Q - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} -\lambda & -1 \\ 1 & -\lambda \end{vmatrix}  = \lambda^{2} + 1 = (\lambda-i)(\lambda+i) \\
\lambda_{1} = i, \vec{x}_{1} = \begin{bmatrix} i \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\
\lambda_{2} = -i, \vec{x}_{2} = \begin{bmatrix} i \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
```

Triangular Matrix

```math
\begin{align}
A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \\

\det (A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 3-\lambda & 1 \\ 0 & 3-\lambda \end{vmatrix}  = (3-\lambda)^{2} \\
\lambda_{1} = \lambda_{2} = 3, \vec{x}_{1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \\
No 2nd independent \vec{x}_{2}
\end{align}
```
